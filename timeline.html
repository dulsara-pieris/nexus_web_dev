The Dawn of Computers

Early computers were huge, used vacuum tubes, and mainly helped governments and scientists with calculations.

Mainframes and Minicomputers

Businesses and universities adopted large mainframes, while minicomputers made computing slightly smaller and more affordable.

The Microcomputer Era

Microprocessors enabled small personal computers like the Altair 8800, Apple II, and Commodore PET, bringing computing to homes and hobbyists.

Home Computers and PC Explosion

IBM PCs, Commodore 64, ZX Spectrum, and Apple Macintosh standardized hardware, brought GUIs, and made computers widely popular at home.

Multimedia and Early Internet

Computers gained multimedia capabilities, CD-ROMs, and networking; Windows 3.1 and early Internet adoption changed how people worked and connected.

Modern Retro Foundations

Laptops became common, open-source systems like Linux grew, and older machines from the 70s–90s started being seen as retro classics.
